MISSION

The DiTi Foundation exists to make technology equitably accessible to communities 
that have historically been excluded from the digital economy. We believe that 
access to technology — and the skills to use it — is a civil rights issue.

---

PROGRAM: TechForward Youth Initiative

Our flagship program, TechForward, is a 12-week intensive digital skills training 
program for young people ages 14–24 in Washington DC's Ward 7 and Ward 8 communities — 
two of the most underserved areas in the city, where broadband access rates fall 
below 60% and youth unemployment exceeds 25%.

The program covers:
- Foundational digital literacy and computer skills
- Introduction to coding (Python and web development)
- AI literacy and responsible technology use
- Professional development and technology career pathways

Since launching in 2022, TechForward has served over 300 young people. 
Program outcomes include:
- 78% program completion rate
- 65% of completers went on to tech-related employment or post-secondary education
- 90% of participants reported increased confidence using technology professionally

Our staff includes three full-time program coordinators, two part-time instructors 
with industry experience, and a network of 15 volunteer mentors from the DC tech sector.

We are seeking funding to expand TechForward to serve an additional 75 youth in 
the next program cycle, with a specific focus on integrating AI literacy into the 
curriculum and providing devices and broadband stipends to participants who lack 
home internet access.
