ORGANIZATION: BrightPath Community Learning Initiative
TYPE: 501(c)(3) Nonprofit | Founded 2018 | Washington, DC
ANNUAL BUDGET: $620,000

MISSION
BrightPath Community Learning Initiative connects underserved Washington, DC residents
with digital skills and technology access they need to participate fully in today economy,
civic life, and educational opportunities.

PROGRAM: TechForward Youth Coding Academy

TARGET POPULATION
Youth ages 13-18 in Washington, DC Wards 7 and 8, where 34% of households lack
reliable home broadband access (DC OCTO, 2023) and high school graduation rates trail
citywide averages by 12 percentage points.

PROGRAM DESCRIPTION
TechForward is a 16-week after-school coding and digital literacy bootcamp running
September through December each year. Participants meet three evenings per week at
learning centers co-located inside Anacostia High School and Thurgood Marshall Academy.

Curriculum covers four modules:
1. Digital Foundations (Weeks 1-3): Internet literacy, online safety, productivity tools
2. Python Basics (Weeks 4-8): Variables, loops, functions, simple programs
3. Web Development (Weeks 9-13): HTML, CSS, JavaScript, personal portfolio
4. Career Pathways (Weeks 14-16): Resume, LinkedIn, tech career exploration

Each cohort serves 50 students. We run two cohorts per year for 200 students annually.
All devices, internet hotspots, and materials are provided at no cost to participants.

PARTNERSHIPS
- Anacostia High School (DCPS): classroom space, student recruitment, academic credit
- Thurgood Marshall Academy PCS: co-location and wraparound support
- NPower: alumni job placement pipeline and industry certification pathways
- Booz Allen Hamilton: volunteer mentors and mock interview support
- DC Public Library: digital inclusion programming integration

OUTCOMES (2023-2024 Annual Report)
- 200 students completed the program (78% completion rate)
- 94% of completers showed measurable improvement on digital skills assessment
- 45% of graduates enrolled in post-secondary STEM program within 6 months
- 85% of participants reported increased confidence using technology for school or work
- 12 alumni placed in paid tech internships through NPower partnership

STAFF
- Program Director: Maria Okonkwo, M.Ed., 9 years youth workforce development experience
- Lead Instructors: 3 certified educators with computer science backgrounds
- Program Coordinator: scheduling, attendance, and family communication
- Data Manager: tracks outcomes using Salesforce NPSP

SUSTAINABILITY
BrightPath funding mix: 38% government grants, 31% foundation grants,
21% corporate partnerships, 10% individual donors. Pursuing multi-year commitments
from two current funders and in conversations with Amazon Future Engineer for 2027.
