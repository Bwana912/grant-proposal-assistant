## 1. Organizational Background

The DiTi Foundation — Digital Innovation & Technology Inclusion Foundation — is a
Washington, DC-based 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization with a singular mission: to make
technology equitably accessible to communities that have historically been excluded from
the digital economy. Founded on the conviction that access to technology and the skills
to use it is a civil rights issue, DiTi Foundation works at the intersection of digital
equity, workforce development, and youth empowerment.

Since 2022, DiTi Foundation has delivered hands-on, practical technology skills
training to young people in Washington DC's most underserved neighborhoods. Our
organizational model is grounded in community trust, industry partnership, and rigorous
program design. We are led by a staff of experienced educators, technologists, and
community advocates who bring both professional expertise and lived experience to our
work.

DiTi Foundation has demonstrated consistent organizational capacity through three
successive program cohorts, sustained community partnerships, and a growing network of
15 volunteer mentors drawn from the DC technology sector. Our track record of results —
including a 78% program completion rate and a 65% post-program employment or education
rate — reflects our commitment to accountability and continuous improvement.

## 2. Statement of Need

Washington DC's Ward 7 and Ward 8 communities face some of the most severe digital
equity gaps in the nation's capital. Broadband access rates in these neighborhoods fall
below 60%, compared to a citywide average exceeding 85%. Youth unemployment in these
wards exceeds 25%, more than double the DC average. These are not abstract statistics —
they represent young people who are being locked out of a technology-driven economy
before they have had a fair opportunity to participate in it.

The populations DiTi Foundation serves — young people ages 14–24 in Ward 7 and Ward 8
— face compounding barriers: lack of devices and reliable internet at home, limited
exposure to professional technology environments, and educational systems that have
not kept pace with the demands of an AI-enabled workforce. Without targeted
intervention, these young people risk permanent exclusion from one of the most
consequential economic sectors of the 21st century.

The urgency of this need has been amplified by the rapid emergence of artificial
intelligence as a core workplace competency. AI and emerging technology literacy are
no longer optional skills — they are foundational requirements for career pathways
in the technology sector. Young people without access to AI literacy training face
an accelerating skills gap that traditional education systems are not equipped to
address at the speed or scale required.

## 3. Project Description

DiTi Foundation's TechForward Youth Initiative is a 12-week intensive digital skills
training program designed to equip young people ages 14–24 with the practical,
career-ready technology skills needed to access employment, higher education, and
entrepreneurship opportunities in the technology sector. The program is delivered in
partnership with community anchors in Ward 7 and Ward 8, ensuring that training is
accessible to youth where they already are.

The TechForward curriculum is structured in three progressive phases. The first four
weeks establish foundational digital literacy and computer skills, ensuring that all
participants — regardless of prior access — enter the program on equal footing. Weeks
five through eight introduce coding fundamentals through Python and web development,
with an emphasis on project-based learning and real-world application. The final four
weeks focus on AI literacy and responsible technology use, professional development,
and career pathway exploration, including direct engagement with technology sector
employers and mentors.

With support from the Horizon Community Foundation, DiTi Foundation will expand
TechForward to serve an additional 75 youth in the next program cycle. This expansion
will include a specific focus on integrating advanced AI literacy content into the
curriculum, reflecting the Foundation's priority areas of AI and emerging technology
literacy and career pathways in the technology sector for underrepresented groups.
Critically, the expansion will also include a device and broadband stipend program
for participants who lack home internet access — directly addressing the device equity
and broadband access priorities the Foundation has identified for this grant cycle.

The program will run from September through November 2026, with recruitment beginning
in July. Program completion will be followed by a structured employer matching process
and a six-month alumni check-in to track employment and education outcomes.

## 4. Goals and Objectives

- **Objective 1:** Enroll and begin training 75 new youth participants (ages 14–24)
  from Ward 7 and Ward 8 in the TechForward program by September 2026.

- **Objective 2:** Achieve a program completion rate of at least 75%, consistent
  with or exceeding prior cohort performance.

- **Objective 3:** Ensure that 100% of enrolled participants who lack home internet
  access receive a device and broadband stipend for the duration of the program.

- **Objective 4:** Deliver a minimum of 20 hours of dedicated AI and emerging
  technology literacy instruction to all program completers.

- **Objective 5:** Place at least 60% of program completers into tech-related
  employment, post-secondary education, or a credentialed training program within
  90 days of program completion.

## 5. Evaluation Plan

DiTi Foundation will track progress toward all five program objectives through a
combination of attendance records, skills assessments, and post-program outcome
surveys. Pre- and post-program digital skills assessments will measure participant
learning gains across all curriculum areas, including AI literacy. Program completion
rates will be tracked in real time, with case managers following up with participants
who miss two or more consecutive sessions.

Post-program outcomes will be tracked at 30, 60, and 90 days via structured
follow-up interviews with program completers. Employment and education placement data
will be collected and verified through employer and institution confirmation. An
independent summary of program outcomes will be compiled and made available to the
Horizon Community Foundation within 60 days of program completion, including a
transparent account of what worked, what did not, and what adjustments will be made
in subsequent cohorts.
