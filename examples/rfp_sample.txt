DIGITAL EQUITY YOUTH PROGRAM GRANT — REQUEST FOR PROPOSALS

Funder: The Horizon Community Foundation
Grant Amount: Up to $50,000
Deadline: Rolling basis
Eligible Applicants: 501(c)(3) nonprofit organizations

---

OVERVIEW

The Horizon Community Foundation invites proposals from nonprofit organizations working 
to advance digital equity and technology access for underserved youth between the ages 
of 14 and 24 in urban communities across the Washington DC metropolitan area.

We are particularly interested in programs that:
- Provide hands-on, practical technology skills training
- Demonstrate a clear and measurable theory of change
- Serve populations with limited prior access to technology resources
- Include a pathway to employment, higher education, or entrepreneurship
- Have demonstrated organizational capacity and a track record of results

---

FUNDING PRIORITIES

This grant cycle, the Foundation is prioritizing proposals that address:
1. AI and emerging technology literacy for youth
2. Broadband access and device equity
3. Career pathways in the technology sector for underrepresented groups

---

PROPOSAL REQUIREMENTS

Proposals must include:
- Organizational background and mission
- Statement of community need with supporting data
- Program description including activities, timeline, and target population
- Measurable goals and objectives
- Evaluation plan
- Brief budget narrative

---

EVALUATION CRITERIA

Proposals will be evaluated on:
- Clarity and specificity of the need statement
- Feasibility and strength of the program design
- Quality and measurability of outcomes
- Evidence of organizational capacity
- Alignment with Foundation priorities
