REQUEST FOR PROPOSALS
Horizon Community Foundation — Digital Equity Initiative 2026
Deadline: June 15, 2026 | Award: Up to $50,000 | Duration: 12 months

OVERVIEW
The Horizon Community Foundation invites 501(c)(3) nonprofit organizations to submit proposals
for its Digital Equity Initiative Grant. This funding cycle prioritizes programs that expand
meaningful internet access and digital skills training for underserved residents in the
Washington, DC metropolitan area, including Wards 7 and 8.

FUNDING PRIORITIES
We will give preference to proposals that:
- Serve residents in historically underconnected neighborhoods (Wards 7, 8, and Prince George County)
- Demonstrate evidence-based curriculum and measurable skill outcomes
- Include partnerships with schools, libraries, or workforce development organizations
- Address barriers beyond device and connectivity including confidence, language, and relevance
- Serve populations including youth (ages 10-24), seniors (60+), or adults seeking employment

ELIGIBILITY REQUIREMENTS
Applicants must:
- Be a 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization in good standing
- Have an annual operating budget of at least $100,000
- Have been in operation for at least two years
- Serve residents primarily within Washington, DC or Prince George County, Maryland
- Not be a government agency or institution of higher education

REQUIRED APPLICATION SECTIONS
1. Executive Summary (max 500 words)
2. Statement of Need (max 750 words) - Local data on the digital access gap
3. Program Description (max 1,000 words) - Goals, activities, timeline, evidence base
4. Measurable Outcomes - At least 3 SMART objectives with data collection methods
5. Organizational Capacity - Track record, staff qualifications, partnerships
6. Budget Narrative - Line-item budget with justification
7. Sustainability Plan (max 300 words)

REQUIRED ATTACHMENTS
- Most recent IRS Form 990 or 990-EZ
- Current year operating budget
- Letters of support from at least two community partners
- List of current board members with affiliations

KEY NUMBERS
- Award amount: $25,000-$50,000 (average award: $38,000)
- Maximum overhead/indirect costs: 15% of total request
- Minimum participants served: 75 individuals during grant period
- Application deadline: June 15, 2026 at 11:59 PM Eastern
- Grant period: September 1, 2026 through August 31, 2027
- Decisions announced: August 1, 2026

WATCH OUT FOR
- Applications missing any required section will be automatically disqualified
- Budget requests exceeding $50,000 will not be considered
- Indirect costs above 15% require written justification
- Letters of support must be on organizational letterhead and signed
- Proposals focused solely on hardware distribution without training are not eligible

REVIEW CRITERIA (100 points)
- Community need and target population: 25 points
- Program design and evidence base: 25 points
- Measurable outcomes and evaluation plan: 20 points
- Organizational capacity and partnerships: 20 points
- Budget clarity and sustainability: 10 points

SUBMISSION
Submit via online portal at horizonfoundation.org/grants by June 15, 2026.
Questions: grants@horizonfoundation.org | 202-555-0190
